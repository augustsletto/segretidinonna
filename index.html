Segreti di Nonna (Grandma's Secrets) is an immersive online resource revealing the cherished secrets behind Neapolitan pizza's rich history while offering an invaluable guide on crafting the perfect, authentic Neapolitan pizza.

Neapolitan Pizza
Neapolitan pizza, tracing its origins to Naples, Italy, is characterized by its distinct soft, chewy crust. Crafted with simple yet high-quality ingredients - San Marzano tomatoes, fresh buffalo mozzarella, basil, and a special dough made from high-protein flour - it undergoes a slow rising process. The result is a thicker, pillowy edge called the cornicione, with a soft, slightly chewy texture that contrasts the crispy and charred exterior. Baked briefly in a wood-fired oven at intense temperatures, around 900°F (485°C), this style of pizza remains slightly moist at its center while offering a delicate yet substantial crust.

Roman Pizza
Roman pizza, originating from Italy's capital, Rome, boasts a thinner and crispier crust compared to its Neapolitan counterpart. Often rectangular or square in shape, Roman pizza is crafted from a dough made with a lower protein flour, resulting in a crisper base. It's commonly sold al taglio (by the slice), allowing for a diverse range of toppings and flavors. The crust is uniformly thin and crunchy, with variations in toppings, from classic combinations to more inventive choices. The overall texture is thinner and crispier, making it distinct from the softer, chewier Neapolitan style.
